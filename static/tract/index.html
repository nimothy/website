This is a story that Jesus told, which is found in Mark's eyewitness account of Jesus' life.

The beginning

"A man planted a vineyard and put a fence around it and dug a pit for the winepress and built a tower, and leased it to tenants and went into another country."

The evil tenants

"When the season came, he sent a servant to the tenants to get from them some of the fruit of the vineyard. And they took him and beat him and sent him away empty-handed. Again he sent to them another servant, and they struck him on the head and treated him shamefully. And he sent another, and him they killed. And so with many others: some they beat, and some they killed."

The death of the heir... The end?

"He had still one other, a beloved son. Finally he sent him to them, saying, 'They will respect my son.' But those tenants said to one another, 'This is the heir. Come, let us kill him, and the inheritance will be ours.' And they took him and killed him and threw him out of the vineyard."

What will the owner of the vineyard do?

"What will the owner of the vineyard do? He will come and destroy the tenants and give the vineyard to others. Have you not read this Scripture: "'The stone that the builders rejected has become the cornerstone; this was the Lord's doing, and it is marvelous in our eyes'?""
Mark 12:1-11

The beginning

"A man planted a vineyard and put a fence around it and dug a pit for the winepress and built a tower, and leased it to tenants and went into another country."

This is the picture of the world. It's Gods world (it is beautiful and it works) and in a sense, He has leased it to us.
(Now of course, the original hearers would have been Jewish so the vineyard to them might be something slightly different but it is still true of us all)
Q: Doesn't it sound beautiful and right?

The evil tenants

"When the season came, he sent a servant to the tenants to get from them some of the fruit of the vineyard. And they took him and beat him and sent him away empty-handed. Again he sent to them another servant, and they struck him on the head and treated him shamefully. And he sent another, and him they killed. And so with many others: some they beat, and some they killed."

Because this world is Gods therefore everything in it belongs to Him. But we wanted to keep this for ourselves and rebel against God. This is the essence of sin.
Q: How would you feel as the vineyard owner or one of His servant?.

The death of the heir... The end?

"He had still one other, a beloved son. Finally he sent him to them, saying, 'They will respect my son.' But those tenants said to one another, 'This is the heir. Come, let us kill him, and the inheritance will be ours.' And they took him and killed him and threw him out of the vineyard."

This is talking about Jesus' deather. Humanity in its evil rebelious state rejected Gods son. Isn't it shocking how the tenants knew exatly what they were doing? Doesn't it make you feel sick?
Can humaity get away with this?
Surely death is the end?

What will the owner of the vineyard do?

"What will the owner of the vineyard do? He will come and destroy the tenants and give the vineyard to others. Have you not read this Scripture: "'The stone that the builders rejected has become the cornerstone; this was the Lord's doing, and it is marvelous in our eyes'?" "

The truth is, we are still in the middle of this story. This is a story from the beginning of time to the very end. Did you know that Jesus has promised to return one day to judge the world that rejected Him? You see, God's plan all along was for Jesus to be rejected so that He can become the beginning of a new way of relating to God.
There are only two options at the end of time: you are part of the rebellious tenants, or you are part of the new vineyard where people give to God His dues.

So- what will it be?

This is just a short overview of some of the amazing things that Jesus said... why not read the rest of Marks gospel, an eye witness account of Jesus' life to find out more?
